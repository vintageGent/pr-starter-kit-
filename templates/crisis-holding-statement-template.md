<!--
  CRISIS COMMUNICATIONS HOLDING STATEMENT

  This template is for use within the first 30-60 minutes of a crisis.
  The goal is NOT to answer every question. It is to show you are aware, empathetic, and in control.
  It buys you time to gather facts. Get this approved by legal if possible.

  Key principles:
  - DO NOT speculate or guess.
  - DO NOT blame anyone.
  - DO NOT lie or mislead.
  - DO express empathy if people are affected.
  - DO state your commitment to finding the facts.
  - DO direct people to a single channel for updates.
-->

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Statement from [Company Name] Regarding [Brief, Factual Description of Incident]**

<!--
  Paragraph 1: Acknowledge the situation and state that you are taking it seriously.
  Show empathy if appropriate.
-->
We are aware of the [incident/reports/issue] involving [e.g., our service outage, a customer report, a security vulnerability]. We are taking this matter extremely seriously, and our primary concern is the well-being of our [customers/users/community].

<!--
  Paragraph 2: State your immediate action and commitment to investigation.
-->
We have launched a full and immediate investigation to understand the facts and the cause of this situation. Our teams are working diligently to confirm all available information.

<!--
  Paragraph 3: Set expectations for future communication. This is critical.
  Give a specific time and place for the next update.
-->
We are committed to being transparent and will share more information as soon as it is confirmed. We will provide our next update within [Timeframe, e.g., 2 hours / by 5 PM EST] via [Official Channel, e.g., our corporate website / our official Twitter account @YourHandle].

<!--
  Paragraph 4: Funnel all inquiries to a single point of contact.
-->
All media inquiries should be directed to [press@yourcompany.com or designated contact].
